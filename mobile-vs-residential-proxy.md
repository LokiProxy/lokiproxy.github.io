## Core Underlying Differences


### 1. Different Network Sources

Residential proxies are based on home broadband networks, with relatively fixed IP addresses and high stability. Mobile proxies, on the other hand, rely on 3G/4G/5G cellular mobile networks, offering greater dynamism and are more difficult for websites to identify.


### 2. Cost and Service Models

Mobile proxies are constrained by base station coverage and the availability of mobile devices, resulting in higher resource scarcity and therefore typically higher pricing. Residential proxies leverage a more abundant pool of IP resources, offering a better balance between cost and stability.


In terms of service models, mobile proxies predominantly adopt dynamic IP mechanisms, supporting short-session or on-demand rotation with greater flexibility and dynamism. Residential proxies, meanwhile, offer both rotating and static options, allowing users to choose freely based on their business requirements.
